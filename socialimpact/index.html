Social Impact Therory

This demo depicts a simplistic model of agents' sentiment change based on the state of their immediate neighbors. The Social Impact Theory was first introduced by social psycologist Bibb Latané.

What you see ...

  1. The graphics shows 2,500 agents aligned on a grid with periodic boundary conditions (i.e. on a torus).
  2. Each agent is in one of two states (red or blue) at any given time.
  3. The pobability to swicth states depends on the number of immediate neighbors in the opposite state.
  4. Agents are more likelky to follow the majority sentiment.
  5. The color saturation indicates the diversity of the neighbors' states. Hereby, saturated colors mean "all agree", faded "all disagree".

How to ...

  1. Click on the graphic to start the simulation.
  2. Click again to pause.
  3. Press any key (while the graphic is on focus) to reset the initial population.

This demo was developed in Processing and runs in this browser with Processing.js. Download the source code.
